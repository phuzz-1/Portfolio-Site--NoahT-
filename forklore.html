Context

The Problem

Many individuals face a lack of confidence and time to cook at home, leading to a reluctance to prepare meals and contribute to unhealthy eating habits. The cooking process itself, recipe selection and planning, ingredients preparation, and cooking can be stressful and overwhelming, negatively impacting the culinary experience.

The Hypothesis

Users would derive greater satisfaction from cooking and adopt healthier eating habits when equipped with the knowledge, time, and ingredients to prepare meals themselves.

Initial Research

Before development I conducted some user research by interviewing potential users. I wanted to find out why people cooked at home and what the biggest pain points that stood in their way.

Key patterns that emerged from initial user research:

  • The primary obstacles to cooking at home revolve around time constraints and the availability of ingredients.
  • Grocery shopping is generally disliked by people.
  • Eating out becomes a common choice when individuals feel fatigued and lack the energy to cook at home.
  • People like to use Google to search for new recipes but the results are inconsistent.

Style Guide

I wanted the design to feel both calming and inspiring, mirroring the transformation of kitchen stress into culinary confidence.
