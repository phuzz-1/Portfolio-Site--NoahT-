Context

Problem

Traditional task prioritization methods often fail to drive motivation effectively, particularly for non-neurotypical individuals. This misalignment can lead to decreased productivity and difficulty in managing tasks. Users need a tool that helps them organize their task in a way that aligns with their personal motivations and keeps them engaged.

Problem

Motive is a productivity app designed to address this issue by reimagining how lists function. By adapting task organization to the user's personal motivations, Motive aims to enhance productivity and make task management more intuitive.

Style Guide

Productivity apps shouldn't feel crowded or overwhelming. They should present and empower the information they hold without getting in the way. The design of Motive reflects this philosophy, utilizing lighter, calming colors and friendly typography to create a welcoming and efficient user experience. The interface is crafted to empower users by making task management straightforward and visually appealing, ensuring that the app supports productivity without becoming a distraction.

Motivation planner

The Motivation Planner is the core function and differentiator of Motiv, designed to assist users in defining their daily objectives and aligning them with their current mood and motivations through a series of guided questions. This approach aims to create a more personalized and effective planning experience, enhancing user engagement and productivity.

However, the current version of the Motivation Planner falls short. The alignment of users' goals with their motivations is not as seamless as it could be, which can lead to a disconnect and reduced effectiveness. To address this, I believe that additional user research is needed. By gaining deeper insights into user behaviors, preferences, and motivational triggers, we can refine the tool to better meet their needs.
